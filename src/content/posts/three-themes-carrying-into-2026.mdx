Becoming an empty nester gave me time to read and think. I've spent the last few months in what I'm calling a reflection rabbit hole. The insights are shaping how I'm looking at the year ahead.

Three themes I'm carrying into 2026.

**Work from roam.**

We live in a small world now. Everyone works from anywhere. That's not news. What gets underestimated is the emotional labor of managing workflows across multiple time zones while trying to stay sane.

The flexibility is real. So is the exhaustion. This year is about building systems that respect both — not just accepting the chaos because the flexibility is worth it.

**Blueprints beat diplomas.**

Practical skills and trades are rising in value while credentials are losing their shine. The electrician who can troubleshoot a transformer failure on-site is worth more than the engineer with the perfect resume who's never touched a live system.

I've watched this play out in infrastructure for years. It's spreading everywhere now. The people who can actually do the thing — fix it, build it, ship it, recover it — are the ones winning regardless of what's on the wall.

**Haste makes waste.**

We're all in a hurry. Ship faster. Deliver sooner. Move quicker. And we're creating future headaches at an alarming rate as a result.

My commitment for this year: slow down. Build better systems and tools to manage information and workflows instead of just moving faster through the chaos. What worked in 2025 won't scale in 2026 without better foundations underneath it.

Looking forward to the work ahead.
