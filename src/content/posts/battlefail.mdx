I love first person shooter games. They were my real gateway drug into gaming and I can't resist a good session of left-clicking on faces to get stress out. I played and loved Battlefield 2, so when Battlefield 3 was announced I was pumped. The launch trailer looked amazing and I couldn't wait — especially because I have the Nvidia NVision 3D glasses and a great Acer GD245HQ 3D monitor to play it in.

I pre-ordered through the new EA Origin system so I could get some perks and waited.

## The launch night

The release was scheduled at 2am on October 25th. I tried to download early when I received an email saying it would be available a day early — no luck, still had to wait until midnight Eastern. No big deal.

So midnight arrives. Here's where it gets funky.

I double-click to play, which kicks up another app that has to validate the release time before it will unlock. Now we're at **3 apps**. The official release time hits, that validator goes away, and then my *web browser launches* to the [Battlelog](http://battlelog.battlefield.com/bf3/) page. I closed it thinking it was weird, but nothing happened.

Turns out I have to install an **ActiveX plugin** to use the site. That's now **5 applications** to play a single game: Origin → game client → validator → Firefox → ActiveX.

## The multiplayer situation

After doing all of this and trying to get into multiplayer (because I like to left-click on real people's faces), I just got a prompt spinning saying it was trying to matchmake. After about 10 minutes of waiting, I gave up and went into campaign mode. The game content itself is amazing — beautiful, impressive 3D, great physics. I enjoy myself for 20 minutes and go to bed.

The next day I come home, feed the kids, do the grown-up things, and try to play again at 7pm — a normal adult gamer attempting to get in half an hour before catching up on email. The login screen greeted me with an account lockout.

![Battlelog account lockout screen](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi9iIcdpU7NKzzGHNqlKRZpM_OXDS68n68PFqAh4tK1R6F3aLvIK5FbE3_EwqFDSNq-0jBeDATujs-OsNpYiQ-Mr8HCQHw695lteZnRxRpnIbyOMzfJ746qE3wSSvNSdUId3ax8LNb3UJM/s1600/Battlefail_login.jpg)

## The verdict

The game itself is great. The execution is a #battlefail.

Those who know me know I'm a very forgiving person and I don't like to complain. But 5 applications to play a game you paid $59.99 for is objectively a mess. The content creators, the licensing team (Origin), and the marketing/social team (Battlelog web interface) clearly did not talk to each other enough before launch.

I don't even mind competition. I don't mind having to log into something other than Steam. But give me a straight shot from desktop to game without loading half my taskbar first.

Now I'm off to figure out how to get my account unlocked so I can left-click on some face. 🙂
