Thanksgiving week. I find myself thinking about what I'm actually grateful for, which means I end up thinking about how I got here.

No college degree. A speech impediment that made professional speaking feel impossible for years. A career in technology that nobody mapped out for me because there was no map.

What there was: people who believed in me before I'd earned it. Managers who saw something I didn't see. Mentors who gave me shots I wasn't fully qualified for yet. My wife Cori, who has been steady through every pivot, every late night, every moment where the path forward wasn't clear.

I've been doing this for thirty years. I still don't take any of it for granted.

What I've learned is that the credential is rarely the thing. What gets you through — what actually builds a career — is showing up, doing the work, and being honest about what you don't know yet. The people who helped me most weren't impressed by what I had on paper. They were watching how I operated when things got hard.

Toastmasters helped me find my voice, literally and professionally. Every role taught me something the previous role didn't. The path only makes sense in hindsight.

If you're building something without a map, the commitment is the map. That's not a motivational poster. That's what thirty years looks like from the inside.

Grateful this week. And every week.
