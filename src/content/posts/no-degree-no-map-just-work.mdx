Thanksgiving week. Most people post the predictable stuff.

Here's what I'm actually grateful for: people who remember where I started.

No college degree. Speech impediment. Every credential I have came from showing up, doing the work, and figuring out what I didn't know. Thirty years later, I'm helping companies navigate infrastructure decisions worth millions. I didn't get here because of a resume. I got here because enough people looked past the gaps.

The people I'm most grateful for aren't impressed by what I've built. They're the ones who know how hard it was to build it.

My wife Cori watched me bet on myself more times than was probably smart. My kids grew up watching it too — seeing that commitment matters more than credentials, and that the credential usually follows the work, not the other way around. Clients trusted me before I had the resume that would have made HR comfortable.

Mentors who saw past the gaps taught me that expertise isn't about where you went to school. It's about what you've solved under pressure, how you made others feel, and how willing you are to keep growing.

So in the spirit of Thanksgiving: if you've got people in your corner who know your whole story and show up anyway, that's earned on both sides. Don't just be grateful for what you've accomplished. Be grateful for the people who believed in the outcome before it was guaranteed.
